4 Days Trip To Darjeeling

created by: Bikram Goswami

After spending few months in lockdown during corona, the plan for Darjeeling was finally ready. We board the train on the early morning of 1st January 2021 from Malda Town(MLDT) and reached New Jalpaiguri(NJP). You may book a hotel room through the agents near the station and depart for Darjeeling.

Darjeeling is a destination that defines serenity in its beautiful language. Once a mountain kingdom, Darjeeling exhibits a religious harmony between Hinduism and Buddhism, has its floors covered with Hindu shrines and Buddhist monasteries. The varied culture of the town reflects its diverse demographic milieu comprising Lepcha, Khampa, Gorkha, Kirati , Newar, Sherpa, Bhutia, Bengali and other mainland Indian ethnolinguistic groups.

Weather At Darjeeling During Winter

Usually, the average temperature remains between 2 to 15-degree centigrade during January. It was mainly because of the weather in Darjeeling in January and to enjoy nature after a long period trapped in lockdown that made us quite bore, the reason we planned on paying a visit to Darjeeling. The weather was too pleasant and made our overall experience extremely wonderful. Although the temperature didn't touch sub-zero, the weather was breezy, a little chilly, and comfortable enough for us to experience Darjeeling to the fullest.

Hotel In Darjeeling

Upon reaching our destination, situated 2100 m above sea level, we searched for a hotel. I request viewers to look for a hotel nearby upon reaching instead of booking online. There will be many hotel options available to you at a lesser price.
We stayed in Sonar Bangla hotel from which Mount Kanchanjanga was visible. It was a fascinating view. The hospitable staff of these hotels welcomed us with arms wide open and the best of services. Hotel Sonar Bangla was our favorite accommodation. As we were about to try local Tibetian and Nepalese cuisine we haven't taken the food options. Any restaurant that has the food served there was in itself drool-worthy. The hotel management helped us by pre-booking the car for sightseeing for all 4 days.

Top Experiences During Darjeeling Trip

There were a few things that we experienced in Darjeeling for the first time and we would surely cherish it for a lifetime. Some of the highlights of our 4 days Darjeeling trip in November is mentioned below.

Day 1

Arrive in Darjeeling. Check into your hotel and freshen up. Later, head over to the Chowrasta Mall. Sit on a peripheral bench, enjoy the ambiance and watch the activities going on. Take a stroll along the scenic Mall road. Check out some old heritage shops like Habib Mullik and Jolly Arts and few others and buy some curios or souvenirs. Walk up to the Observatory Hill behind the mall and go to the famous Mahakal Mandir on top and enjoy the views and sunset. In the evening, stroll along Nehru road down from the Mall and all kinds of shops on both sides. Have your diner in one of the restaurants like Glenary's or Shangri-La or Fiesta.

1. Chowrasta Mall Road

Day 2

Early morning, we experienced the bitter cold that was touching our bones. Before exploring, we went to a Tibetan restaurant and had an authentic dish called Chicken Chop Suey.

Later head off for the 5-point local tour that takes about 4-5 hours and covered the following places:

1. Peace Pagoda and Japanese Temple

2. Happy Valley Tea Estate

3. Padmaja Naidu Zoological Park

4. Himalayan Mountaineering Institute(HMI)

5. Bengal Natural History Museum(BNHM)

6. Rangeet Valley Ropeway(RVR)

After exploring, we had our lunch at Glenary's restaurant and bar and in the diner, we had our local delicious Thukpa and momos with special chutney.

Day 3

Go for the early morning tour covering the sunrise from Tiger Hill, followed by Rock Garden and Batasia Loop.

1. Tiger Hill

2. Rock Garden

3. Batasia Loop

We were back at our hotel by 10:00 AM, had our lunch, and relaxed for a while. Later headed toward Mahakal Mandir and taken a stroll along the scenic Mall road. Check out some old heritage shops and bought some souvenirs. Don't forget to buy the special blended Darjeeling tea full of flavour and aroma.

4. Mahakal Mandir

As it was our last night, we want our diner to be memorable. So went for one of the most rated restaurants, Glenary's Restaurant and Bar in Darjeeling.

5. Glenary's Restaurant and Bar

Day 4

We took a car and made a Day trip to Mirik. Visit Gopaldhara Tea Garden, get the traditional handcrafted Darjeeling tea and then Mirik Lake, with a lovely view and a water body and enjoyed the lake, boating and horse-riding, and finally Simana, a freeway Indo-Nepal border with a fascinating view of Manebhanjan and mountains, and also try their special mini-wai wai.

1. Gopaldhara Tea Estate

2. Simana ViewPoint

4. Mirik Lake
